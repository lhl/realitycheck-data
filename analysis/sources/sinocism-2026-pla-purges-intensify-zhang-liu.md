## Stage 3: Dialectical Analysis

### Steelmanned Argument
When a regime needs to remove a highly connected figure, it must (a) legitimize the decision internally and (b) deter resistance. Extreme propaganda language (“undermined the chairman responsibility system”) is a way to frame the removal as a defense of party supremacy rather than mere graft. Fast confirmation can reduce rumor-driven instability by quickly establishing a single authoritative narrative.

### Strongest Counterarguments
1. Propaganda language is not truth-tracking: it can be generic moral panic used for any target.
2. Timing may be administrative, not strategic; inference from speed is weak without comparative baselines.

### Synthesis Notes
Sinocism is valuable for pinning the official signaling language: political authority, cliques, and “party commands the gun” logic. In synthesis, treat this as higher-confidence than specific leaked allegations (detention timing, family corruption details).
